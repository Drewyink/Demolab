🔵 Medicare Advantage (Part C)
Medicare Advantage plans are approved by CMS and must meet all federal requirements. Each plan has a CMS Contract Number (H#), a Plan Benefit Package (PBP) number, and is assigned to a CMS Region. Plans earn STARS ratings that determine quality bonus payments. Medicare is always primary payer except in specific MSP situations such as active employment or ESRD.
🟢 Medicaid Managed Care
Medicaid is jointly funded by federal and state governments using the FMAP formula. Managed care organizations contract with states to serve Medicaid members on a capitation basis. EPSDT (Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic, and Treatment) requires comprehensive coverage for members under 21. CHIP extends coverage to children in families up to 200% of the Federal Poverty Level.
🟣 Dual Eligible (Medicare + Medicaid)
Dual eligible members are enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid simultaneously. Medicare is primary payer. Medicaid is secondary and wraps around Medicare to cover cost-sharing, long-term services, and benefits not covered by Medicare. D-SNP plans serve full duals with coordinated benefits. The COB sequencing must be set correctly or claims will pay in the wrong order.
⚡ How Programs Connect to Claims
Every government program flows through the same adjudication chain as commercial plans. Members must be enrolled in a program-linked plan. Benefits, fee tables, and provider contracts must all be configured for the government program line of business. MSP rules determine payer sequencing. Dual eligible claims require COB with Non-Duplication or Medicare Supplement COB method.

CMS Contract Information (Medicare Plans)
CMS Contract Structure
Every Medicare Advantage plan requires a CMS Contract Number (H number), a Plan ID combining H number and PBP, and a Plan Benefit Package number. The H number identifies the sponsoring organization. The PBP identifies the specific plan within that organization. Example: H1234-001 where H1234 is the contract and 001 is the PBP.
STARS Rating and Quality
STARS Rating Impact
CMS rates every Medicare Advantage plan on a 1 to 5 STARS scale based on HEDIS quality measures, CAHPS member satisfaction, and health outcomes. Plans with 4 or more STARS earn quality bonus payments that increase capitation rates. Plans below 3 STARS for three consecutive years face enrollment sanctions and potential contract termination.

Dual Eligible Configuration
Dual Eligible — How It Works in Facets
Full duals are enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid. Medicare is always the primary payer. Medicaid wraps around Medicare as secondary. In Facets, you must configure COB on the member record with Medicare as Plan 1 and Medicaid as Plan 2. The COB method for dual eligible members is typically Non-Duplication — Medicaid pays only what Medicare did not cover. Partial duals receive Medicaid assistance with cost-sharing only.
Medicare Secondary Payer (MSP)
⚠ MSP Rules — Critical Configuration
Medicare Secondary Payer rules require Medicare to pay AFTER another insurer in specific situations. MSP applies when the member has employer group health coverage through current employment (EGHP), is covered under a working spouse employer plan, has workers compensation, has no-fault or liability insurance, or has ESRD and is within the 30-month coordination period. Incorrectly setting Medicare as primary when MSP applies is a federal compliance violation.
