Medicare Advantage (Part C)
Medicare Advantage plans are approved by CMS and must meet all federal requirements. Each plan has a CMS Contract Number (H number), a Plan Benefit Package (PBP) number, and is assigned to a CMS Region. Plans earn STARS ratings that determine quality bonus payments. Medicare is always primary payer except in specific MSP situations.
Medicaid Managed Care
Medicaid is jointly funded by federal and state governments using the FMAP formula. MCOs contract with states to serve members on a capitation basis. EPSDT requires comprehensive coverage for members under 21. CHIP extends coverage to children in families up to 200% of the Federal Poverty Level.
Dual Eligible — Medicare plus Medicaid
Dual eligible members are enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid. Medicare is always primary payer. Medicaid wraps around Medicare as secondary and covers cost-sharing, long-term services, and benefits not covered by Medicare. D-SNP plans serve full duals with coordinated benefits. COB sequencing must be configured correctly or claims pay in the wrong order.
How Programs Connect to Claims
Every government program flows through the same adjudication chain as commercial plans. Members must be enrolled in a program-linked plan. Benefits, fee tables, and provider contracts must be configured for the government LOB. MSP rules determine payer sequencing. Dual eligible claims require COB with Non-Duplication or Crossover method.

CMS Contract Information
CMS Contract Structure
Every Medicare Advantage plan requires a CMS Contract Number (H number), a Plan ID combining the H number and PBP, and a Plan Benefit Package number. Example: H1234-001 where H1234 is the contract and 001 is the PBP.
STARS Rating and Quality
STARS Rating Impact on Payment
CMS rates every Medicare Advantage plan on a 1 to 5 STARS scale based on HEDIS quality measures, CAHPS member satisfaction, and health outcomes. Plans with 4 or more STARS earn quality bonus payments that increase capitation rates. Plans below 3 STARS for three consecutive years face enrollment sanctions and potential contract termination.

Dual Eligible Configuration
Dual Eligible — How It Works in Facets
Full duals are enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid. Medicare is always the primary payer. Medicaid wraps around Medicare as secondary. In Facets you must configure COB on the member record with Medicare as Plan 1 and Medicaid as Plan 2. The COB method for dual eligible members is typically Non-Duplication — Medicaid pays only what Medicare did not cover.
Medicare Secondary Payer (MSP)
MSP Rules — Critical Configuration
MSP rules require Medicare to pay AFTER another insurer in specific situations: active employer group health coverage (EGHP), working spouse employer plan, workers compensation, no-fault or liability insurance, or ESRD within the 30-month coordination period. Incorrectly setting Medicare as primary when MSP applies is a federal compliance violation.
